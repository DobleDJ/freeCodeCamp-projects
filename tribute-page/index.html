Steve Jobs

Pioneer of the personal computer revolution

Here's a time line of Steve's life:

  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco, California.
  • 1972 - Jobs attends Reed College in Portland, Oregon, but drops out after six months.
  • 1974 - Steve Jobs gets a job at Atari, a video game company, where he meets Steve Wozniak.
  • 1976 - Jobs and Wozniak found Apple Computer, Inc. in Jobs' parents' garage.
  • 1977 - The Apple II, Apple's first successful personal computer, is launched.
  • 1980 - Apple goes public and becomes one of the most successful initial public offerings in history.
  • 1983 - Apple's Lisa computer is launched, the first with a graphical user interface.
  • 1984 - Apple launches the Macintosh, a revolutionary personal computer with a mouse and graphical user interface.
  • 1985 - Jobs is fired from Apple after a power struggle with the company's then-CEO, John Sculley.
  • 1986 - Steve Jobs co-founds NeXT Inc., a computer and software development company.
  • 1986 - Purchases The Graphics Group, which eventually becomes Pixar Animation Studios.
  • 1996 - Apple acquires NeXT Inc., and Steve Jobs returns to the company as an advisor and later becomes interim CEO.
  • 1997 - Jobs becomes Apple's CEO permanently and leads the company's restructuring.
  • 2001 - The iPod is launched, a digital music player that revolutionizes the music industry.
  • 2007 - Steve Jobs introduces the iPhone, a device that combines a mobile phone, iPod, and communications device into one, ushering in the era of the smartphone.
  • 2010 - The iPad, a tablet that redefines mobile computing, is launched.
  • 2011 - Dies at the age of 56.

"The people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do."

-- Steven Paul Jobs --
